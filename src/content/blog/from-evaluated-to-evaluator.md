# From Being Evaluated to Building the Evaluator

Fundraising for years changes the way you see startup infrastructure. PerPitch came from that shift: moving from founder-side friction to system design for the ecosystem itself.

## The Cerebralx Years

I spent nearly five years building Cerebralx, a brain-computer interface venture. That meant pitching—constantly. 50+ investor conversations. Accelerator applications. Demo days. Follow-up emails.

Each pitch taught me something about how founders are judged. Not the theory from blogs, but the reality of decision-making under uncertainty.

## What I Observed

The evaluation process I experienced was:

- **Inconsistent**: Same pitch, different reactions depending on mood and context
- **Pattern-matching heavy**: Investors looking for signals that matched past winners
- **Static**: Snapshots in time, not trajectory over time
- **Social-proof dependent**: Warm intros valued over cold outreach, regardless of merit

This isn't criticism. Early-stage evaluation is genuinely hard. But the process can be better.

## The Friction Becomes the Product

Every frustrating pitch experience became a data point. I started asking:

- What would systematic founder evaluation look like?
- How do you capture trajectory, not just snapshots?
- Can behavioral patterns predict outcomes better than pedigree?
- What infrastructure would make this process fairer for everyone?

These questions became PerPitch.

## Building the System

PerPitch isn't about automating VC decisions. It's about giving evaluators better tools:

- **Structured data** instead of gut feeling
- **Behavioral signals** instead of resume pattern-matching
- **Trajectory analysis** instead of static snapshots
- **Observable metrics** instead of opaque impressions

The goal isn't to replace human judgment. It's to inform it.

## The Meta-Lesson

Living through a problem teaches you more than reading about it. The specific friction of fundraising—being on the evaluated side—gave me insight no market research could.

This pattern repeats: the best infrastructure often comes from people who lived the pain.

## Where This Goes

PerPitch is still early. But the direction is clear: better founder evaluation benefits everyone. Founders get fairer assessment. Investors get better signal. The ecosystem gets more outliers discovered.

The journey from Cerebralx to PerPitch wasn't planned. But it was inevitable once I saw the problem clearly.

The lesson: lived experience is the best product research. Sometimes you're building the thing you wish existed.
